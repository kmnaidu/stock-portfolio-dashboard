## How to Set Up to Receive Money in India

### Bank Account Setup (Critical)

1. **Open a current account** (not just savings) at:
   - HDFC Bank, ICICI Bank, or Axis Bank
   - Tell them: "I'm freelancing internationally, need foreign remittance"
   - They'll set up FIRA/FIRC for inward remittance

2. **Get a USD/Foreign Currency account** (optional but recommended for tax efficiency):
   - HDFC FCNR or Wise India account
   - Receive in USD, convert when rates are good

### Payment Methods From Foreign Clients

| Method | Speed | Fees | Best For |
|--------|-------|------|----------|
| **Wise (formerly TransferWise)** | 1-2 days | 0.5-1% | Most senior freelancers |
| **PayPal** | Instant | 4-5% | Avoid for large amounts (high fees) |
| **Direct Wire (SWIFT)** | 3-5 days | $25-40 + bank fees | Large payments only |
| **Toptal Payoneer** | Auto | Built-in | Toptal has its own system |
| **Stripe (via Indian biz)** | 2-7 days | 2-3% | Direct invoicing clients |

**Recommendation:** Open Wise account TODAY (free). Every freelancer uses it.

### Indian Tax Reality (Don't Skip This)

You'll be classified as **Self-Employed/Professional**. Tax treatment:

#### Option A: Sole Proprietorship (Simplest)
- Use your PAN as business identity
- File ITR-3 or ITR-4 (Presumptive Taxation Scheme)
- **Presumptive 50% taxation:** Just declare 50% of income as profit, pay tax on that
  - Earn ₹50 lakh/year? Declare ₹25 lakh as taxable income
  - Tax: ~₹5-6 lakh on that, total effective tax ~12%
- **No GST required** if income < ₹20 lakh/year (₹1.66 lakh/month)
- **GST required** above ₹20 lakh/year — register and charge 18% (clients pay it on top)

#### Option B: Register as a Sole Proprietorship Business
- Apply for **GSTIN** at gst.gov.in (free, 7-15 days)
- Apply for **Professional Tax** in Telangana
- Open business current account
- Issue formal invoices

**Recommended for your level:** Register for GST since you'll cross ₹20L easily.

### Monthly Bookkeeping (Use Apps, Don't Do Manually)

## Common Mistakes To Avoid

### Pricing Mistakes
❌ Charging too low to "get the first client" — you'll be stuck there
❌ Pricing per project without scope — leads to scope creep nightmares
❌ Hourly under ₹3,500 for senior work — undervalues you

### Contract Mistakes
❌ Working without a written agreement
❌ Starting work before partial payment received
❌ No clause for scope changes
❌ Letting clients "pay after delivery" for the full amount

**Always:** 50% upfront, 50% on delivery. Or weekly invoicing for hourly work.

### Operational Mistakes
❌ Not tracking time accurately (lose money)
❌ Mixing personal and business bank accounts
❌ Forgetting to invoice (cash flow disaster)
❌ Not paying advance tax quarterly (penalty)
